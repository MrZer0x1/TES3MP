TES3MP Water FX Update
======================

Implemented in water_fragment.glsl (and synced to files/, cmake/files/, manual/files/, .github/files/):

1) Surface shadows on water
- Shadow factor now affects the visible water surface, not just specular highlights.
- Added soft preservation of reflections in shadow so water keeps readable reflections instead of becoming flat black.
- Shadow response is angle-aware and lighter at high fresnel angles.

2) Time-of-day water haze
- Added lightweight distance + horizon based haze over the water surface.
- Dawn/dusk gets a warmer veil.
- Night gets a subtle cool haze.
- Implemented as a cheap color pass with smoothstep/mix only.

3) Floating luminescent particles
- Added procedural surface motes directly in the water shader.
- They drift slowly, flicker softly, and are strongest at dusk/night.
- Effect fades with rain and distance to protect performance.
- Restricted to above-water camera view so it does not pollute underwater rendering.

Performance notes
- The luminescent motes are shader-procedural, not a separate engine particle system.
- They are range-limited and time-of-day gated.
- The haze is extremely cheap.
- The biggest added cost is the night-only mote pass, but it is clamped to short range and low density.

Files changed
- files/shaders/water_fragment.glsl
- cmake/files/shaders/water_fragment.glsl
- manual/files/shaders/water_fragment.glsl
- .github/files/shaders/water_fragment.glsl

Recommended in-game test cases
- Noon with shadows ON: check visible shadowing on water surface near piers/rocks/shore.
- Sunset: verify warm haze near the horizon.
- Deep night: verify soft green-cyan drifting motes on calm water.
- Rainy night: verify motes fade down and rain ripples still dominate.

Notes
- This update does not create engine-level particle emitters; it simulates luminescent floating particles in the water shader itself.
- If you want true independent 3D particles next, that should be done as a separate VFX/particle-system pass.
